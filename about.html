About our Project

Purpose

Air quality affects our daily lives, even when we are unaware of it. This project is intended to raise awareness of air quality issues and promote our users to make informed decisions about their outdoor activities, health, and overall lifestyle. Staying informed about air quality is both convenience but also safety. Unsafe conditions can lead to various health conditions, like respiratory issues.

This web application will allow users to input their geographic location such as their city, state or zip code using data from reliable APIs to deliver accurate and up-to-date air quality information for a particular area. The data will be presented using an interactive map, as well as using charts as a visualization showing pollutant levels. Our system will also help display tips for health safety based on the air quality index.

APIs

This is the list of APIs that our application uses

  • IQ Air
    • Provides access to real-time air quality data for different locations around the world. IQ Air also gives coordinates to help make a visual map.
  • Leaflet JS
    • Leaflet is the leading open-source JavaScript library for mobile-friendly interactive maps. This will be used to create an interactive map that updates with the user's zip code.
  • ZipCodeAPI
    • This is the API used to convert zipcode information entered by the user to city and state data.

Air Quality - Good or Bad?

Air quality is separated into categories, commonly referred to as the Air Quality Index. The following is an explanation of the different AQ levels based on the chart:

  1. Good:
    • This air quality level is indicated by the green section, which ranges from 0 to 50. Air quality in this range is considered healthy for everyone.
  2. Moderate:
    • The yellow section, ranging from 51 to 100, indicates moderate air quality. At this level, air pollution may be a concern for some sensitive groups, such as people with respiratory conditions.
  3. Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups:
    • The orange section, ranging from 101 to 150, indicates that the air quality is unhealthy for sensitive groups, such as children, older adults, and people with certain health conditions.
  4. Unhealthy:
    • The red section, ranging from 151 to 200, indicates that the air quality is unhealthy for everyone, not just sensitive groups. At this level, everyone may experience health effects.
  5. Very Unhealthy:
    • The purple section, ranging from 201 to 300, indicates that the air quality is very unhealthy, and everyone may experience more serious health effects.
  6. Hazardous:
    • The maroon section, ranging from 301 to 500, indicates that the air quality is hazardous, and everyone may experience severe health effects.
